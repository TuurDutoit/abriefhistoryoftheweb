More info

On 7 march 1876, Alexander Graham Bell registers a patent for "the method of, and apparatus for, transmitting vocal or other sounds telegraphically...". In simpler terms: the telephone. He wasn't the only one working on it, of course, but he was the first to get one working and to register a patent.
In 1877, he founded the Bell Telephone Company, the company that would run the phone lines for the forseeable future and would - as we now know - play a vital role in our story.
